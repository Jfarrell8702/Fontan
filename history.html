Conception to Present

  1. 1943 Starr et al. demonstrated that destruction of the right ventricular wall with diathermy did not result in significant venous hypertension in dogs
  2. 1949 Rodbard and Wagner showed that the right ventricle (RV) can be bypassed in humans by anastomosing the right atrial appendage (RAA) to the main pulmonary artery with sufficient pressure, there is antegrade flow into the main pulmonary artery (MPA)
  3. 1954 Warden et al. performed staged operations. They first created tricuspid stenosis to produce right atrial enlargement and hypertrophy and then anastomosed the RAA to the pulmonary artery [6]. They rightly predicted that this could be the potential treatment for tricuspid atresia.
  4. 1958 William Glenn introduced a paper including a very elaborate description of an off-bypass end-to-side anastomosis between the right pulmonary artery (RPA) with the superior vena cava (SVC) through a right thoracotomy, transposition of the great arteries and pulmonary stenosis. The SVC-RA junction was only ligated.
  5. 1971 Francis Fontan and Eugene Baudet published a landmark article titled ‘surgical repair of tricuspid atresia’. The Fontan operation was introduced into the clinical arena
  6. Present 60,000 people have Fontan right now and projecteed to increase by 2030, 55% adults, 17% adolescents, 28% children
